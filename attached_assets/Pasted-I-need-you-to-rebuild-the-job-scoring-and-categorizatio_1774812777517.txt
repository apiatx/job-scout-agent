## THE NEW SCORING SYSTEM

Every job that reaches Claude scoring must receive a composite score from 0-100 made up of these weighted components:

COMPONENT 1 — Role Fit (30 points max)
Does the role title and seniority match what the user is looking for?
- Exact match on target role title: 30 points
- Close match (same level, slightly different title): 20 points
- Related role that makes sense given resume: 10 points
- Wrong level (too junior or too senior): 5 points
- Completely wrong role type: 0 points

Read target roles from user settings table. Do not hardcode.

COMPONENT 2 — Company Quality Score (25 points max)
This is the intelligence layer. Claude must evaluate the company itself, not just the job.

25 points — Pre-approved company on user's companies list AND company is currently in a high-growth, high-momentum phase (recent funding, expanding sales team, strong product-market fit, AI-era tailwind)

20 points — Pre-approved company on user's companies list, solid established company

15 points — Not on user's list but passes isCompanySafe() check AND shows strong hiring momentum signals

10 points — Not on user's list, safe company, no particular momentum signal

0 points — Pure SaaS with AI disruption risk, or company shows layoff signals, or company is in decline

To evaluate company momentum for non-pre-approved companies, Claude must consider:
- Is this company expanding its sales org right now or contracting?
- Does this company sell something that AI makes MORE valuable, not less?
- Is there recent funding, IPO activity, major partnership, or customer expansion news?
- Is the product in a category that is growing because of AI infrastructure demand?

Use the Gemini API which is already integrated to search for company momentum signals when evaluating non-pre-approved companies. Query: "[COMPANY NAME] hiring expansion funding 2025 2026 sales growth"

COMPONENT 3 — Compensation Fit (20 points max)
Read minimum base pay from user settings table.

20 points — Salary listed and clearly meets or exceeds user minimum base
15 points — Salary listed and is within 10% below user minimum (close enough)
10 points — No salary listed but company is known to pay above user minimum for this role type (use Gemini to estimate if salary_estimates table has no cached data)
5 points — No salary listed and company comp is unclear
0 points — Salary listed and is clearly below user minimum base

For OTE estimation when salary is not listed:
Use the salary_estimates table if a fresh estimate exists (under 7 days old).
If not, use Gemini to search: "[COMPANY NAME] [JOB TITLE] base salary OTE 2025 2026 Glassdoor LinkedIn"
Store result in salary_estimates table for future use.
Display both base AND OTE on every job card — if estimated show "~" prefix.

COMPONENT 4 — Location Fit (15 points max)
Read locations from user settings table. This is a SCORING factor not a hard filter — do not exclude jobs based on location, just score them lower.

15 points — Remote US with no state restriction, or explicitly in one of user's preferred locations
10 points — Hybrid with home office in user's preferred region
5 points — Requires relocation to a state not in user preferences but is a Top Target company
0 points — On-site only in a location far from user preferences with no remote option

COMPONENT 5 — Territory Intelligence (10 points max)
This is new. If the job title contains a geographic territory indicator (Southeast, Northeast, Mid-Atlantic, SLED, Federal, etc.):

10 points — Territory matches user's preferred locations AND user's resume shows relevant experience in that territory
7 points — Territory matches user's preferred locations
3 points — Territory does not match but role could transition to user's region
0 points — Territory is a hard geographic requirement far from user preferences

If no territory is detected in the title, give 7 points by default (neutral).

## CATEGORY THRESHOLDS

After calculating total score 0-100, assign category:

TOP TARGETS: score 80-100
Requirements to qualify:
- Component 1 (Role Fit) must be at least 20/30
- Component 3 (Compensation) must be at least 10/20
- Component 2 (Company Quality) must be at least 15/25
- These are jobs the user should apply to immediately

FAST WINS: score 65-79
- Strong fit on role and compensation
- Company may not be pre-approved but passes safety check
- Good opportunity, apply within the week

STRETCH: score 45-64
- Either the role is slightly off, pay is unclear, or location is not ideal
- Worth reviewing — user may have context that bumps it up
- Do not discard these — some of the best opportunities are here

PROBABLY PASS: score 0-44
- Multiple components are weak
- Only show these if the user explicitly wants to see them
- Default to hidden or collapsed

## COMPANY MOMENTUM INTELLIGENCE — THE BRAIN LAYER

This is the most important addition. Before running Claude scoring on any job, run a company momentum check for every unique company in the current scout run.

Create function getCompanyMomentum(companyName: string): Promise<MomentumScore>

MomentumScore:
{
  score: number (0-25, feeds directly into Component 2),
  signals: string[],
  warning: string | null,
  cached: boolean
}

Cache in a company_momentum table:
CREATE TABLE IF NOT EXISTS company_momentum (
  id SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
  company_name TEXT NOT NULL,
  momentum_score INTEGER NOT NULL,
  signals JSONB,
  warning TEXT,
  created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);

Cache is valid for 48 hours.

For pre-approved companies in user's companies table:
- Start with base score of 20 (user already vetted them)
- Use Gemini to check for positive signals: recent funding, product launch, sales team expansion, AI strategy announcement = boost to 25
- Check for negative signals: layoffs, revenue miss, product discontinuation, leadership exodus = reduce to 15 or lower
- Query: "[COMPANY NAME] news 2025 2026 hiring layoffs funding growth"

For non-pre-approved companies from JobSpy:
- Start with base score of 10
- Run isCompanySafe() check — if false, score drops to 0 immediately
- If safe, use Gemini to check momentum signals
- Strong positive signals: boost to 15-20
- No signals either way: stay at 10

POSITIVE momentum signals (boost score):
- Series B or later funding in last 18 months
- IPO filed or rumored
- Major enterprise customer announced
- Expanding sales team (multiple AE roles posted simultaneously)
- Strategic partnership with hyperscaler (AWS, Google, Azure, NVIDIA)
- Product launch in AI infrastructure, networking, semiconductor, or energy space
- Revenue growth announced
- Analyst recognition (Gartner Magic Quadrant, Forrester Wave)

NEGATIVE momentum signals (reduce score or flag warning):
- Layoffs announced in last 12 months
- CEO/CRO departure without replacement announced
- Revenue miss or guidance cut
- Category disruption by AI (especially if their core product is being replaced)
- Multiple negative Glassdoor reviews about comp cuts or quota changes
- Company acquired and going through integration (uncertain territory)

Display warning flags on job cards when negative signals are detected. Example: "⚠️ Recent layoffs reported" or "⚠️ Leadership transition" in a muted orange color.
